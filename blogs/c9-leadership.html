Search Inside Yourself: The Unexpected Path to Achieving Success, Happiness(and World Peace)

Chapter 8 - Leadership and Social Skills


What is your experience of compassionate and leadership?

Compassionate

I have had several years on this earth learning and although I thought myself to be naturally compassionate. This skill can sometimes be difficult to maintain and from my understanding mindfulness has help me to understand myself and better control my emotions. Through my journey of mindfulness I have taken to the time to center myself and everything I have experienced up until now has been beneficial.

Leadership

Having worked in a few leadership roles in the past I can certainly understand being compassionate, through cognitive, affective and motivational is an important valuable skill to master. I learned alot things about myself while in these work roles about relating to other people as well as ensuring I thought about what I said and how it would affect others.

Do the multiplying goodness meditation. How did you find it?

Everytime I meditate I enjoy the experience more, so after this exercise I knew I would feel great. It was a struggle to think of everyone as good because I have long known it a character flaw of mine and it has got into trouble many times. Thinking about goodness and everyone being good, when I knew this to be false. I found it was a challenege, so I had to say it aloud.

Do the Tonglen meditation. How did you find it?

From the perspective of a mountain the experience was illuminating and different. Taking in negativity and thinking about it. However, exhaling it all out was easy and far more relieving. Using visualisation is not a new concept. After watching another channel four story last week about the crisis in Myanmar so imagine human suffering and pain was manageable to understand. In return having alot of positive quotes around the house and keep a vision board that I assess and look at often. What I learned was taking on negative thought was only made easy because I knew it would end in positive thoughts.


How does the SCARF model apply to your Phase 0 experience so far? How will it apply to bootcamp?

Status, - I believe the completion of this course will open new doors to new career. I have also found it feels good to tell friends and family about this exciting new career path.

Certainty - As the chapter suggests, uncertainty generates “error responses” in the brain that cannot be ignored until they are resolved. My main fear is that I would not remember the knowledge and I knew that I could not retain it all. My certainty came from knowing that I would learn more as I went through the process and I have accepted this as apart of my learning process.

Autonomy - There were countless times when I felt helpless and lost. I felt this was apart of the learning process for me. To understand how I manage stress and trouble shoot through it and come out the otherside wiser.

Relatedness - It was great being able to connect with other students at EDA. I did relate well with others as we shared each others experiece. This made me feel at ease knowwing other students had experience some of the same emotions and concerns.

Fairness - I strongly believe that although I disapline with fairness, I have alway believed also that sometimes life is not unfair. Through my lifetime I have learned to endure some hardships as I am sure we all have. This has made me more resilent and tolerant.

Have a difficult conversation. How did it go?

I recently had to make a call I was not looking forward to making. I knew the discussion was going to be very difficult. After running through the conversation through my head several times and I felt prepared. Once I was ready and careful with the words I used and offered my thanks when the other person replied. I was happy when the situation was resolved and apologies were shared all around. Peace was again restored.
