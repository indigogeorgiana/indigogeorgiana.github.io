Chapter 7 - Developing Empathy Through Understanding and Connecting with Others


Have you tried using any of the insight and techniques for growing trust and sincerity? How did it go?

In the weeks leading up to this chapter I have used the informal practice of empathetic listening on several occassions in my household. I am known within the household as the peace keeper on occassion I have had to make the call to resolve incidents. Which involes listening to both sides of an argument, then of course working out how to solve it. This means calmly talking through the problem from each persons perspective then compromising. I often deploy this technique, to highlight a persons strengths, it makes people feel good about themselves. It may not 100% be the technique described but was the only type of instance I could come up with where geniuine and positive praise was used recently.


How did you find the loving kindness meditation?

It was good to have to extend praise, kindness out to loved ones. Up until now a lot of the meditation has been about self, which of course is good for self care as that is important. But it was also nice to take the time thinking of others and their lives and extending empathy to a loved one.


Complete the political awareness exercise here in writing.

1. Think of a difficult situation from your present or past, when there was some conflict or disagreement, something real, something that has some meaning and potency for you.

I am by no means a radical green peace activist but I believe in reducing waste where ever I am able and know that I can improve on reducing my own and my families carbon footprint. However, most recently a topic that has been close to my heart is the environment and the effects humans as a species has on mother earth. Several weeks back now there was a post on Channel 4 News face book page about Faroe island life and annual mass hunt of pilot whales. It was filmed from an outsiders len's, not demonizing their way of life. Which in my personal opinion the best way to views on the matter.

2 Describe the situation as though you are 100 percent correct and reasonable. Do that either in writing or by talking about it in a monologue.

When I first layed my eyes on the video and the hunt, at first I felt anger. As I would like seeing Donald Triumphs son proudly displaying his hunting trophies of lion's and other animals in South Africa. It made me sick. I remember sitting with that rage for a while watching and wondering as Pamela Anderson said on that very piece of documentary footage. "Why do you need to kill whales?" Then, secondly honestly my eyes welled up I could not believe like the Japanese whom slaughter and have been allowed to do for so long. This is not right, this should not be allowed any longer. I began searching through the comments and found alot of different points of view. I choose to reply with something less upsetting and hateful. "It might be a way of life for these people. It may even be tradition. Regardless there has to be a point where we as a people recognize how much irreversible damage we each cause to the food chain. Thus affecting the generations after us. What kind of world are we leaving behind?"

3. Now describe the situation as though the other person is (or the other people are) 100 percent correct and reasonable. Do that either in writing or by talking about it in a monologue.

This documentary is going to be a must watch for me. On the other side of the argument, the islanders that live on this island live off what they can catch where ever possible. Choosing to reduce the amount of processed or store brought food. For them it is how they have always lived. The doctor that takes care of the Faroes has recently seen an increase in mercury and plastic containimenants over recent years and the islanders are noticing the effects. Are they too feeling a sense of loss in their on traditions? How will they go to survive with the noticable environmental changes? They too will eventually the feelings of loss, how will some of these families survive the changes to the earth? The more I ventured into the video I found that the video was not entirely about the hunt but about a way of life to the environment far more intimate than what I had thought, it did make me see things from another point of view.
