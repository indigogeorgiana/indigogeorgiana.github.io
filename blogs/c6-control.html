Chapter 5 - Developing Self-Mastery

When programming, what are some of your triggers?

When I hit a wall with respects to my code, I have thoughts of self-doubt and my internal dialogue takes over and hi-jacks my confidence. This is ultimately and usually before I've had a chance to problem solve, trouble shoot or research anything I might have missed in my learning. The other time I experience this is when I have overworked myself. In my pursuit to learn I find I tend to push myself to grasp a method I may struggle to understand.

What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

If I am being honest with myself, I knowingly place a lot of pressure to perform and learn to a fault at times. I accept that this is a part of who I am, I like to be successful in all I set to achieve. I believe I do not like to fail, though I rationally believe it is a part of learning and a part of everyday life. I tend to hold myself to a higher standard because of my past failings.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

Today while working through Sprint 6, I attempted the SNRR exercise. 1. Stop - Stopping all thought was quiet hard to do, I find that I think very quickly, a little too quickly. And trying to halt my thought process was very difficult. It took a couple of tries. 2. Breathe - Taking in a long deep breathe, inhaling and exhaling I thought of nothing else but breathing this gave me the mental space away from thinking about solving the problem. 3. Notice and 4. Reflect - Next I stopped to notice my emotions I felt a little nervous and anixous. I like to finish things especially my train of thought. Then I reflected on where I was and why I felt that way I did. 5. Respond - After a few short counts I let my new problem solving plan play out.
