Chapter 3 - Extending the Benefits of Mindfulness beyond Sitting

What did you learn from your mindfulness conversation?

For my little experiment this week I asked my neighbour over for coffee with the idea of not telling her what I was going to do. My neighbour and I have a great friendship and we enjoy a catch up over coffee. However much a good listener I think myself to be, this week was an opportunity for me to really test my ability at being patient. I practised listening without judgement as she talked nodding and encouraging as she talked. It really wasn't that hard to do I also found that I like to hear what others have to say and observe them openly. I find I learn a lot about a person this way. As the book suggested talking continuously for three minutes was a hard for mr to maintain. I found when it was my turn to talk I rather enjoyed being heard.

Which of the three techniques will you use to sustain you practice?

As the author suggests "sustaining a mindfulness practice often lasts only a few months. It's like starting an exercise regime."

For myself I find this to be true. Weekly life at home is luckily very structured. Even though I manage home work, and mindfulness for 2 minutes a day is great but I would like to increase this time. I am up early most mornings so adding this to my regular routine is welcomed.
