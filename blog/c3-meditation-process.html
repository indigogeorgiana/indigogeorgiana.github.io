Chatper 2 - Searching inside yourself: The Unexpected Path to Achieving Success, Happiness(and World Peace)


How did the 'process over product' concept affect the way you tackled the site redesign and rebuild?

Process over product made perfect sense and was very eye opening. It would have made more impact if this information was placed in Sprint 2. This week I realised I am quiet a visual person, nothing would have stopped me from spending my own personal time tweaking the website. It's like a fidget spinner, I just couldn't leave it alone. I don't think of it was time wasted. In hindsight however I will admit that I could have simplified the website more as and used the box model process we learnt in the Udacity Tutorials. These things alone could have saved me some time in the Sprint 3 re-design.

What did you think about meditation before reading chapter 2?

Lightheartedly, I thought meditation was something Tibetan monks had mastered exceptionally well or something wealthy people did as a part of a self-prescribed wellness regime. It honestly held very little appeal for me personally, or so I thought.

In truth what I did know was that if meditation was honed and mastered correctly, there was some kind of, (well sort of) intangible sense of inner peace, just how it could really be measured I didn’t know. I definitely understood that happiness, mental wellbeing, and an overall outwardly appearance of peace were some of the side effects. What I have come to learn is that there is also a lot more benefits of mindfulness meditation.

What new things have you learnt about meditation?

"Slagter’s study showed that after just three months of intensive and rigorous training in mindfulness meditation, participants can significantly reduce their attentional blink. The theory is that with mindfulness meditation training, one’s brain can learn to process stimuli more efficiently, hence after processing the first salient target, it still has the mental resources to process the second."

Another study that stood out to was a study by Antoine Lutz showed that Buddhist meditators are able to generate high-amplitude gamma brain waves. Resulting in highly effectiveness in memory, learning, and perception.

Both of these fact astounded me if by practicing mindfulness meditation there was a marked effect on the brain then what it could do for me? What Dev Academy Bootcamp from my own understand was full-on an experience that would likely challenge my mind intellectually, mental and maybe on some level physically. Preparing myself in advance seemed a sound and worthy cause.

Did any of the suggested meditation techniques stand out to you?

Opened or closed eyes during meditation is optional. That surprised me, so I decided to try it. From my own perspective after trying it I did find the visual sitmulation was distracting. Closed eyes I found kept me focussed and on track. It is my hope that by making mindful meditation a part of my daily routine the least What I could benefit was happiness, increase, higher clarity and I quote "the possibility of upgrading the operating efficiency."

Any other musings?

Honestly, I had not realized just how difficult it would be to turn off my internal dialogue, switch off and practice just being. Everyday before I have a busy day planned, I could hear the washing beeping at me reminding me it was ready to hung on the line. But I am assured by the writer that it may take some practice and time to manage.
