Chatper 2 - Searching inside yourself: The Unexpected Path to Achieving Success, Happiness(and World Peace)

What did you learn from your mindfulness conversation?

However much a good listener I maybe it was a really good exercise in being patient and not speaking and being mindful of others. As the book suggested talking continuously for for three minutes was a hard to maintain. I found that I like to hear what others have to say and observe them openly, I find I learn a lot about a person this way. They could be friends, family, neighbours so this test was somewhat easy either.

For my experiment I asked my neighbour over for coffee with the idea of not telling her I was going to do. My neighbour and I have a great friendship. Over a hot drink I realised that she had a lot to say, I learnt a lot more about her through this exercise. I practised listening without judgement as she talked nodding and encouraging as she talked. I found when it was my turn to talk I enjoyed being listened too.

Which of the three techniques will you use to sustain you practice?

As the author suggests "sustaining a mindfulness practice often lasts only a few months. It's like starting an exercise regime."

For myself I find this to be true. Weekly life at home is luckily very structured. Even though I manage home work, and mindfulness for 2 minutes a day is great but I would like to increase this time. I am up early most mornings so adding this to my regular routine is welcomed.
